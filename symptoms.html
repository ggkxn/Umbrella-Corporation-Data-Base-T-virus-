T-VIRUS / CLINICAL SYMPTOMATOLOGY & HOST BEHAVIOR

CONFIDENTIAL: Detailed classification of biological and neurological degradation following T-Virus infection. Progression is categorized into three distinct phases.

PHASE I: INITIAL VIREMIA (4 - 24 HOURS POST-EXPOSURE)

  • [Fever] Profound increase in core body temperature (hyperpyrexia).
  • [Photophobia] Extreme sensitivity to light; dilated, bloodshot pupils.
  • [Cognition] Disorientation, severe headache, and episodic short-term memory loss.
  • [Behavior] Increased aggression and profound paranoia in isolated cases.

PHASE II: TISSUE NECROSIS (1 - 3 DAYS POST-EXPOSURE)

  • [Motor] Significant decrease in fine motor control (ataxia). Involuntary muscle spasms.
  • [Metabolism] Extreme decrease in metabolic rate, leading to deep coma-like states.
  • [Oral] Advanced necrosis and discoloration of oral mucosa and soft palate.
  • [Sense] Complete loss of pain perception (analgesia). Heightened olfactory response.

PHASE III: REANIMATION & HOSTILE STATE (3+ DAYS)

  • [Cerebral] Complete cessation of higher brain function; brainstem activity only.
  • [Integumentary] Extreme pallor; skin is cold, brittle, and partially sloughed off.
  • [Behavior] Immediate, relentless pursuit of living tissue. Incapacity for reason or recognition.
  • [Viral Load] Virus load peaks, making the host a primary vector for aerosol and contact transmission.
